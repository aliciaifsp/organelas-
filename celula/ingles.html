Is a chicken egg made up of a single cell?

explanation

A chicken egg can be considered a single cell because, in its initial form, it is formed by a cell called an oocyte, which contains all the genetic material and nutrients necessary for the development of the embryo. Despite its large size, it functions as a single cell capable of generating a complete organism.

Parts of the Egg

The eggshell protects the interior from impact and allows gas exchange, while the egg white nourishes and protects the embryo with its proteins and water. The chalazae serve to keep the yolk in the center of the egg and protect it from impact, acting as a support and “shock absorber".

Cell Division

Yolk (Vitelo):  The most nutrient-rich part, containing proteins, fats, vitamins, and minerals essential for embryo development. 

Embryonic Disc: In fertilized eggs, cell division and embryo development occur. This area is where the nucleus and cytoplasm of the oocyte are concentrated. 

Air Chamber: A space that forms inside the egg, providing the air necessary for the embryo in the final stage of incubation, just before hatching.

Vitelline membrane: A structure of protein layers that surrounds the egg yolk, serving as a protective barrier against the yolk mixing with the white and as a defense against microbial contamination.
